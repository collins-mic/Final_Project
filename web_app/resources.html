Understanding Your Emotions

A simple guide to what you're feeling and why it matters.

What Are Emotions?

Emotions are your body's way of responding to the world. They are not "good" or "bad"—they are simply signals. Like the dashboard in a car, they give you important information.

Feeling sad might signal you've experienced a loss. Feeling anxious might mean you're worried about an upcoming challenge. Feeling happy signals that something is going well.

The first step to managing your feelings is to simply notice them without judgment. This app is built to help you do just that.

Meet Your Emotions

Anxiety

Anxiety is your body's natural alarm system. It's a feeling of worry or fear about what's to come. A little bit can be helpful (it makes you study for a test!), but a lot can be overwhelming. It's often your mind trying to protect you from a future threat.

Sadness

Sadness is a normal response to loss, disappointment, or difficult situations. It's a signal that you care deeply about something or someone. Allowing yourself to feel sad is a necessary part of healing and moving forward.

Anger

Anger is often a response to feeling threatened, treated unfairly, or frustrated. It's an active, high-energy emotion. It can be a powerful signal that one of your boundaries has been crossed or that a situation is unjust.

Joy

Joy is a feeling of great pleasure and happiness. It's a signal that your needs are being met and that you are experiencing something positive. Noticing what brings you joy is just as important as noticing what brings you sadness.
